# Recruitment of mental health nurses

Published: [Newsletter 15 July](https://mailchi.mp/22ffe8ad992c/5-minutes-against-stigma-5254829?e=[UNIQID])
<br>Data source: [Mental Health Services Monthly Statistics](https://digital.nhs.uk/data-and-information/publications/statistical/mental-health-services-monthly-statistics)

The number of nurses in mental health services is increasing, latest data by NHS Digital reveals. 

Between March 2019 and March 2021, there have been **2,443 new nursing roles**. 

<img src="total-nursing-workforce.png" width=70%>

Over two thirds of these nurses have been recruited for community services.

<img src="community-nurses.png" width=70%>

There are national strategies in place for expanding the mental health workforce. In 2019, the The Mental Health Implementation Plan outlined that 27,640 more mental health staff would be needed by 2023/24 to achieve the ambition of delivering evidence-based services to an additional 2 million people. 4,220 of the new roles are planned for nursing.
